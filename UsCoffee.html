Did You Know?

Barako coffee was first planted in Batangas, Luzon, Philippines by Spanish friars in the 18th century. The variety spread all over Batangas and was exported to the United States in the 1800s. At one point in the 19th century, the Philippines was the 4th largest coffee producing country in the world.

Coffea arabica is a prized and globally celebrated coffee species that represents the pinnacle of quality in the world of coffee. Originating from the high-altitude regions of Ethiopia, Arabica is renowned for its smooth, nuanced flavor profile and aromatic qualities.

Coffee Excelsa also known as Coffea liberica var. dewevrei, is a unique and distinct coffee variety renowned for its bold and exotic flavor profile. Originating from West and Central Africa, particularly in countries like Liberia, Sierra Leone, and Guinea, Coffee Excelsa stands out among other coffee beans due to its large, asymmetrical shape and robust taste.

Coffee Robusta scientifically known as Coffea canephora, is a robust and resilient coffee species that plays a significant role in the global coffee industry. Originating from central and western sub-Saharan Africa, Robusta has spread to various tropical regions, with major producers including Vietnam, Brazil, and Indonesia.

Espresso a concentrated and robust coffee extraction, is a cornerstone of coffee culture worldwide. Originating from Italy, this intense brew is crafted by forcing hot water through finely-ground coffee under high pressure, resulting in a velvety shot with a layer of golden crema.
